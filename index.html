Survival Blueprint

Advice from Seniors: The Survival Kit

Uncensored developer wisdom. The 6 critical mistakes first-year freshers make and how to avoid them easily.

Core Senior Wisdom Principles

1. Build your network early

Make genuine friends with both day scholars and boarders. Connect with seniors; they hold previous years' quiz blueprints, question papers, and resource drive drives!

2. Focus deeply on logic over rote memory

Memorizing code from slides gets you zero points inside labs. Learn core data structures, practice algorithms on compiler consoles, and solve problems from base fundamentals.

3. Approach Faculty for Research Opportunities

Amrita faculty are highly active in scientific research. If you are interested in AI, Cryptography, or Material Science, book consultation hours and ask to help on papers. Co-authoring is a fantastic CV highlight!

6 Common Freshers Traps & How to Avoid

❌ Trap 1: Keeping doubts locked up

Seniors say: Speak up! Waiting until exam night is a direct path to low relative grade averages.

❌ Trap 2: Ignoring essential soft skills

Placements filters assess verbal communication and team coordination just as much as your core scripts.

❌ Trap 3: Chasing points over competence

High GPA marks are useless if you struggle heavily inside whiteboard developer screenings.

❌ Trap 4: Joining no student club

Join cultural or development teams! It provides outstanding leadership experience and life parameters.

❌ Trap 5: Terribly poor weekly tracking

Failing to write daily records means you'll have 10 messy files to submit during midterm weeks.

❌ Trap 6: Failing to connect with seniors

Never hesitate to drop a 'Hi' to a second or third-year student. We are completely eager to guide you!
